Case study

One project, told properly — the problem, the work, and what changed.

Ziva Health — clinical data for a health-monitoring platform

Multi-tenantRPM / vitalsFHIR & RESTGraphQLWebhooks

The problem

Ziva Health runs a multi-organization health-monitoring platform — for clinics, employer wellness programs and membership plans — where providers track patients' real-time vitals: heart rate, blood pressure, sleep, blood oxygen, glucose. To give providers the full picture, Ziva also needed each patient's existing clinical record from whatever EHR their clinic already ran — and every clinic ran a different one.

What I did

I built a multi-tenant integration service in Django that puts every EHR behind a single request/response contract. Per-clinic configuration templates map each vendor's schema without new code, and webhook-driven sync keeps records current. Ziva integrates once; each EHR — Healthie, Elation and the rest — is just an adapter behind the same contract.

The result

Onboarding a clinic on a new EHR became a configuration change, not a code change. One contract across the adapters meant fewer integration bugs and sharply faster onboarding — and providers now see EHR clinical history right alongside live device vitals.

Questions buyers ask

The things worth knowing before a first call. If yours isn't here, just ask.

How long does one EHR integration take?

A single read-only integration against a documented FHIR or REST sandbox is usually 2–4 weeks. Bi-directional sync with write-back, or a vendor with a slow sandbox-approval process, runs longer. After a short scoping call I give you a concrete, milestone-by-milestone estimate before any commitment.

Do you handle HIPAA, and will you sign a BAA?

Yes. I build with PHI isolation, audit logging, least-privilege access and secure secret handling by default, and I'm happy to sign a Business Associate Agreement before any protected health information is involved.

How do you price projects?

Milestone-based and fixed-scope. We agree the deliverables up front and you pay per working milestone — not per hour, not per calendar week. Longer or open-ended engagements can move to a monthly retainer.

Can you work US business hours?

Yes. I'm based in Pune (IST) and keep a reliable working overlap with US business hours for standups, calls, reviews and launch windows.

Which EHRs have you worked with?

eClinicalWorks, Epic/OpenEpic, Athena, NextGen, Nextech, Healthie, Elation and OncoEMR — with the operation-by-operation detail in the coverage map above. If your EHR isn't listed, the adapter pattern makes onboarding a new one routine.

Can you join an existing team and codebase?

Yes — I can own an integration end-to-end as a standalone deliverable, or work inside your repo and conventions alongside your engineers. Whatever keeps the project moving.
